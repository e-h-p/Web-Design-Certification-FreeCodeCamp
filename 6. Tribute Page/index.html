A Champion of the Oppressed: Maulana Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani

Maulana Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani was a prominent Bengali politician. His political tenure spanned the British colonial India, Pakistan and Bangladesh periods. He was popularly known by the honorary title Mozlum Jananeta (Leader of the Oppressed) for his lifelong stance advocating for the poor.

  • 1880 - Born in Dhanpara village, Sirajganj district, British India (present-day Bangladesh)
  • 1917 - Joins the Khilafat Movement
  • 1930 - Elected President of the Muslim League
  • 1946 - 1952 - Actively campaigns for the rights of peasants and workers
  • 1952 - Plays a key role in the Bengali Language Movement
  • 1953 - Founds the National Democratic Front (NDF)
  • 1954 - Becomes President of the East Pakistan Peasants' Association
  • 1955 - Leads the effort to rename the Awami Muslim League to simply "Awami League"
  • 1960s - Emerges as a vocal critic of the Ayub Khan regime
  • 1969 - Leads mass protests against the Ayub Khan government
  • 1971 - Supports the Bangladesh Liberation War
  • 1976 - Passes away in Dhaka, Bangladesh
